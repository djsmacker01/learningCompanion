GDPR Compliance

Last updated: January 1, 2024

GDPR Compliance: Learning Companion is committed to protecting your privacy and complying with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).
1. What is GDPR?

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a comprehensive data protection law that gives EU residents greater control over their personal data. It applies to all organizations that process personal data of EU residents, regardless of where the organization is located.

2. Our Commitment to GDPR

We are committed to:

  • Transparency: Clear communication about how we use your data
  • Accountability: Taking responsibility for data protection
  • Privacy by Design: Building privacy into our systems
  • Data Minimization: Collecting only necessary data
  • Purpose Limitation: Using data only for stated purposes
  • Storage Limitation: Keeping data only as long as needed
3. Your Rights Under GDPR
3.1 Right to Information

You have the right to know:

  • What personal data we collect
  • How we use your data
  • Who we share it with
  • How long we keep it
  • Your rights regarding your data
3.2 Right of Access

You can request a copy of all personal data we hold about you, including:

  • Account information and profile data
  • Study sessions and progress tracking
  • Social interactions and communications
  • Analytics and usage data
  • Any other personal information we process
3.3 Right to Rectification

You can request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data:

  • Update your profile information
  • Correct study session data
  • Modify your preferences
  • Update contact information
3.4 Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)

You can request deletion of your personal data when:

  • Data is no longer necessary for its original purpose
  • You withdraw consent
  • Data was processed unlawfully
  • You object to processing and there are no overriding legitimate grounds
3.5 Right to Restrict Processing

You can request that we limit how we process your data when:

  • You contest the accuracy of data
  • Processing is unlawful but you prefer restriction to deletion
  • We no longer need the data but you need it for legal claims
  • You object to processing pending verification of legitimate grounds
3.6 Right to Data Portability

You can request your data in a structured, machine-readable format:

  • Export your study data
  • Download your content and notes
  • Transfer data to another service
  • Receive data in a common format (JSON, CSV)
3.7 Right to Object

You can object to processing of your data for:

  • Direct marketing purposes
  • Profiling and automated decision-making
  • Research and analytics
  • Any other legitimate interests
3.8 Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making

You have rights regarding automated processing:

  • Not to be subject to automated decision-making
  • To human intervention in automated decisions
  • To express your point of view
  • To contest automated decisions
4. How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise your GDPR rights, you can:

  • Use our self-service tools: Access your data through your account settings
  • Contact our Data Protection Officer: Email dpo@learningcompanion.com
  • Submit a request form: Use our online data request form
  • Contact support: Reach out to our support team
5. Data Processing Lawful Basis

We process your personal data based on the following lawful grounds:

Purpose Lawful Basis Data Types
Account Management Contract Performance Name, email, profile data
Study Tracking Contract Performance Sessions, progress, analytics
Social Features Consent Friends, groups, messages
AI Recommendations Legitimate Interest Usage patterns, preferences
Marketing Consent Email, preferences
Security Legitimate Interest IP address, device info
6. Data Transfers

We may transfer your data outside the EU/EEA with appropriate safeguards:

  • Adequacy Decisions: Transfers to countries with adequate protection
  • Standard Contractual Clauses: EU-approved data transfer agreements
  • Binding Corporate Rules: Internal data protection policies
  • Certification Schemes: Privacy Shield or similar frameworks
7. Data Protection by Design

We implement privacy by design principles:

  • Data Minimization: Collect only necessary data
  • Purpose Limitation: Use data only for stated purposes
  • Storage Limitation: Keep data only as long as needed
  • Accuracy: Ensure data is accurate and up-to-date
  • Security: Protect data with appropriate measures
  • Transparency: Be clear about data practices
8. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach, we will:

  • Notify Authorities: Report to supervisory authority within 72 hours
  • Inform Users: Notify affected users without undue delay
  • Document Incident: Keep records of the breach and response
  • Take Remedial Action: Implement measures to prevent recurrence
9. Children's Data

We have special protections for children's data:

  • Age Verification: Verify age before collecting data
  • Parental Consent: Require consent for children under 16
  • Limited Processing: Process only necessary data
  • Enhanced Security: Additional protection measures
